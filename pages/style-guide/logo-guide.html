    Logo Guide

    An integral part of BYU-Idaho's brand is the university's logos. Specific guidelines for the use of each logo helps maintain the clarity and distinctiveness of the brand.

    Logos

    Official Logo

    The official university logo is the Two-line Monogram. The acronym BYU is found in capital letters resting above the word Idaho, separated by a thin horizontal line. The CES Board of Directors, developed this logo to closely match both BYU and BYU-Hawaii's logos. Always use official logo artwork.

    Do not try to recreate the logo.

    Limited Use Logos

    In addition to the official logo, other marks were developed for BYU-Idaho: the Medallion, the Seal, and the One-Line Monogram. Use of these logos is restricted and requires prior authorization from the executive office (PEG).

    Official Logo Treatment

    When using the any BYU-Idaho logo, you are representing the university. Using the official logo, the Two-Line Monogram, requires attention to some specific guidelines.

    Logo Color

    Several color variations of the official logo may be used:

    Black logo on white background

    Blue logo on white background

    Gray logo on white background

    White logo on black background

    White logo on blue background

    White logo on gray background

    The official color scheme for the logo is BYUI blue on white with a thin gray horizontal line dividing BYU and Idaho.

    Logo Minimum Size

    The height of the logo should never be smaller than 3⁄8", or approximately 36 px wide.

    Logo Clear Space

    Always include "clear space" on all sides of the logo. Clear space should be determined by the height of the letter B found on the top left corner of the logo. This space should be kept clear of any other elements.

    Logo Placement

    Avoid placing the BYU-Idaho official logo next to other logos. This can imply endorsement.

    Logo Treatments to Avoid

    Stretching

    Distorsion

    Recreation of logo artwork

    Enlarging individual elements

    Changing to unapproved color schemes

    Adding text

    Changing logo fonts

    Adding texture

    Adding/removing elements

    Moving individual elements

    Rotation

    Logo Signature

    Official departments, offices, and programs of the university may associate their name with the official BYU-Idaho logo to form a signature. This is recommended for use on various office documents, marketing materials, and signage. It also may be used on merchandise and apparel.

    Primary logos and departments signatures are available for download at www.byui.edu/identity. Logos are available in blue, black, and white. If you cannot find your department's signature, please contact University Relations rather than attempting to create your own.

    Departments / Offices

    Departments and offices use their respective signatures when promoting and identifying themselves. Departments signatures can be use for:

    • Department/office signage
    • Letterhead, memos, etc.
    • Promotional material
    • Department/office merchandising
    • Brochures
    • Magazines
    • T-shirts
    • Inter-office communications and collateral
    • Opening or closing video slate

    Multiple signatures types are available for use.

    Basic logo signature: Includes official logo and department name

    Horizontal: Official logo on the left with the department name on the right and a thin vertical bar in between.

    Contact logo signature: Includes official logo and department name stacked above department contact information

    Horizontal: Official logo on the left with department name and contact information on the right and a thin vertical bar in between.

    Stacked: Official logo stacked on top of department name and contact information then left aligned.

    Large logo signature: Includes a larger official logo and department name.

    Department Name: Official logo with department name centered beneath.

    Programs

    Programs associated with an official department or office may use the large-logo signature with their program name.

    Program signatures can be used for:

    • Merchandise
    • Apparel
    • Signage
    • Other printed or digital marketing material

    Large-logo signature types for programs are:

    Program Name: Official logo, program name centered beneath

    Sub-organization: Official logo, sub-organization name centered beneath

    Sub-organization and Program Name: Official logo, sub-organization centered beneath, followed by program name centered beneath.

    Upon request, University Relations will provide artwork for program signatures. Please do not recreate them or combine the BYU-Idaho logo with your program name using different fonts. Certain templates require particular signatures. See Marketing Style Guides for more information.

    Internal Logo Policy

    BYU-Idaho has established a recognizable, worldwide brand due in part to the primary use of the official logo. University departments, offices, and programs are encouraged to help maintain a unified visual experience and leverage the brand's equity by focusing on the official, Two-line Monogram.

    By university policy, the creation and use of internal logos for universitydepartments, offices, programs, services, etc., is not permitted.

    Sub-brands

    While internal logos are not allowed, there are still ways to differentiate your organizationfrom others. See Sub-brand Style Guide for more information.

    Other Logos

    Merchandising Logos

    Merchandising logos - placed on promotional goods to be bought, sold, or distributed in a retail manner - play a limited part in the BYU-Idaho brand. Permission to use these logos may be obtained from University Relations.

    Historical Logos

    Historical logos are not a part of the current BYU-Idaho brand. In rare instances, historical logos may be used for merchandising purposes with permission from University Relations.
