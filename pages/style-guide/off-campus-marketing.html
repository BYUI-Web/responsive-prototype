    Off-Campus Marketing

    Certain elements have been established to ensure that the university's brand is recognizable and familiar. These elements are consistemt across all off-campus communication materials. They are also to be used for on-campus institutional messages (e.g. Executive Office, Honor Code reminders, registration deadlines, etc.), as well as those that represent BYU-Idaho in the off-campus community.

    Required brand elements include:

    • A black box with the official BYU-Idaho Two-Lined Monogram logo (logo box)
    • A blue identifying bar (blue bar)

    The required branding elements are very simple, and allow for considerable flexibility.
    This guide ensures the proper use of the blue bar and logo box.

    Logo-box

    The logo box is a white, official logo center on a black square.

    Color
    The logo box should be solid black. No textures, shoadows or gradients are allowed.

    Logo Placement
    The logo-box should be a black square with the official logo in the center. Always include "clar space" - an area free of other elements - on all sides of the logo. This can be determined by the height of the letter "B" found on the top left corner of the logo.

    Outline

    To set it apart from a dark background, a think gray stroke must be given to the logo box. Thickness: .25 point CMYK: (0,0,0,50) RGB: (150,150,150) Hex: (959595) If the medium is too large for this thickness to be adequate, bring the stroke size up to .5 point.

    Size
    To determine the size of a logo box, measure 1/7th of the shortest side of the design. See below for exceptions and additional information:

    Too thin or long:
    When communication materials (e.g. web ads, banners) are too thin or long for the suggested 1/7 ratio, the logo box may fill the height or width of the design and blue becomes the background color.

    Very Large or Small:
    When communication materials are very large or small, use judgement to size the logo box such as to allow for good visibility.

    Minimum Size:
    Avoid scaling the logo box smaller than 50px or 3/4 inches

    Logo box placement
    Where possible, the logo box should be placed in a corner one box width from either corner or centered along the blue bar.

    Bleed
    Any printed artwork should include a bleed of at least .175". Bleed is the zone outside the trim area or in other words the area to be trimmed off. Have artwork extend into the bleed ensures the artwork prints to the edge of the paper. The logo box should be extended into the bleed to create a rectangle so that when cut to size the logo box remains a square.

    Blue bar

    Color
    Only use the official BYU-Idaho Blue. No textures, shadows or gradients are allowed. (Refer to the Color Style Guide as needed)

    Content
    Avoid placing content in the blue bar unless there are unavoidable size constraints. (See "Height" section.)

    Height
    When possible, design the blue bar to be 1/2 the height or width of the logo box. Exceptions exist for when the design size is too small. See below for details:

    Blue bar size
    Make the blue bar the height of the logo box and add content. When this is necessary place the logo box in the left or right corner.

    Fill background with blue
    When BYUI Blue is used for the background, follow the logo box guidelines mentioned in the "Logo box placement" section.

    Placement
    Grouped with the logo box, the blue bar can be used anywhere on the four sides of the document.
