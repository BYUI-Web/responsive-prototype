    Photography Guide

    BYU-Idaho has always been a uniquely student-focused institution. To help convey this message, photos of bright, energetic students play a major role in the university's promotional imagery. Promotional photos should exude optimism and warmth. They should also be beautiful, professional, collegiate and spiritually appropriate.

    Student-Focused Photos

    Successful student-focused promotional photos highlight the educational process by showing students engaged in realistic collegiate settings.

    Some examples include:

    Showing BYU-Idaho students acting for themselves rather than being acted upon.

    Illustrating participants fully engaged in academic or student life scenarios.

    Providing enough landmarks to distinguish that the models are on the BYU-Idaho campus when shooting outdoors.

    Indicating an active faculty presence.

    Campus-Focused Photos

    Use beautiful photos of the campus grounds and buildings to connect audiences with and promote the university.

    Including some student traffic in photography adds life and vitality. Photographing building architecture can be very effective, as can photos set in other gathering places, such as the Ricks Gardens, Spori Quad and Amphitheatre Quad.

    Off-Campus Photography

    When shooting photography off campus, follow the same principles as on-campus photography. While the subject matter may be different, we want to maintain the same feeling.

    Some examples of off-campus photography may be:

    • Expeditions.
    • Internships.
    • Online students.

    Model Guidelines

    Seek out models who:

    • Adhere to the Honor Code in their personal life.
    • Exemplify the BYU-Idaho dress and grooming standards.
    • Highlight the diversity of our student body.

    Each model must fill out a model release form

    How to Obtain or Produce Branded Photography

    Custom Photos: University RelationsPhoto team at (208) 496-2080

    Existing Campus Photography: Available to anyone with a campus username and password. Use only for university-related projects.

    PC: My Computer --> Map Network Drive --> Type in \\joseph\prphoto$

    Mac: Finder --> Go --> Connect to Server --> Type in smb://joseph.byui.edu/prphoto$

    Stock Images: Use the principles found in this style guide to effectively choose images. Be sure to purchase the right photo permissions and copyright licenses for the application of the images you choose. Whenever you can, use imagery provided by the university. If stock imagery is necessary, use it with discretion.

    Do-it-yourself: Use the principles found in this style guide when producing your own work.

    Whether your own original photography or the work of someone else, photos of campus may not be used for commercial gain.

    Using images from the internet without permission from the artist is not permitted.

    To use photos of LDS temples, seek permission from the Church's Intellectual Property Office. If the temple is not the main focus of the image, permission is not required.

    Approved photos of BYU-Idaho's President, vice-presidents, and their families can be obtained through University Relations.

    See the Photo Tips Guide for additional guidelines and resources.
