Plan

Determine what your message is, the best way to communicate it, and what action you desire when others recieve the message.

These advertisements can be placed in the academic buildings on the University. They can be placed on pre-approved boards or on their own department board. Can be placed on other departments' boards if they recieve permission first.

Strengths:

Physical ads can be seen by those waling on-campus.

Challenges:

Crowded Channel. Must be placed in specific, pre-approved locations throughout the campus.

Estimated Cost: 11x17 $1.15

Create

Use your passion and knowledge to combine the different parts of your message together in a way that will inspire, inform, or invite others.

  • Download the poster template.
  • Download your organization's logo and place it in the respective spot on the template.
  • Design your media using the template provided and submit your final design as a PDF to Advertising & Marketing for approval. Use the On-campus marketing Approval Submission Form to submit your material.

Approval

Get the approval or verification that your marketing material meet the guidelines and polices of the Unviersity to move forward.

An Advertising & Marketing Assistant will email you back within 2 business days. Please inlcude the following information in your submission:

  • PDF file of your poster.
  • How many posters you are planning to print.
  • Where you will be placing them. (For Academic Buildings, please list the biildings that you plan to place your posters).

Once you have your approval, you can print out the posters. You will then bring them to 362 Biddulph Hall, where the Advertising & Marketing Assistants will stamp them. If you have requested the digital stamp, you can skip this step.

Publish

Print or export your media and distribute your message through official BYU-Idaho marketing channels.

Place media in any of the designated locations.

  • Austin: First floor bulletin board and poster rails.
  • Benson: Poster rails on first and second floors. Bulletin board on second floor.
  • Clarke: Bulletin boards on all three floors.
  • Hart: Poster rails on second floor only. Bulletin board in east hall, second floor.
  • Kimball: Poster rails on first and second floors, south end of building. To use Kimball bulletin boards requires permission from the adjacent offices.
  • Kirkham: Bulletin boards at south entrance, first and second floor in west hallway.
  • Manwaring Center: Bulletin boards on Mall Level.
  • McKay: Requires approval of the library administrative office.
  • Ricks: Poster rails on all floors. To use Rick's bulletin boards requires permission from the adjacent offices.
  • Romney: Poster rails on all three floors. Some open bulletin boards on first and second floors.
  • Smith: Poster rails on all floors. To use Smith bulletin boards requires permission from the adjacent offices.
  • Snow: Poster rails in halls on first and second floors. To use Snow bulletin boards requires permission from the adjacent offices.
  • Spori: Rails are reserved for artwork. Bulletin boards in south entrance and stairways.
  • Taylor: Bulletin boards on first floor.
